Wings of Fire

by Arun Tiwari

Every common man who by his sheer grit and hard work achieves success should share his story with the rest for they may find inspiration and strength to go on, in his story. The Wings of Fire' is one such autobiography by visionary scientist Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, who from very humble beginnings rose to be the President of India. The book is full of insights, personal moments and life experiences of Dr. Kalam. It gives us an understanding on his journey of success.

Harry Potter

by J.K.Rowling

Harry Potter is a young orphan who discovers he is a wizard and attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. With the help of his friends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, Harry battles the evil Lord Voldemort, who killed his parents and seeks to gain control of the wizarding world. Throughout the series, Harry faces numerous challenges and discovers more about his family and magical powers, eventually leading to the defeat of Voldemort.

The Subtle Art of not Giving a Fu*k

Mark Manson

The Subtle Art of Not Giving a Fck is a self-help book written by Mark Manson. The book advocates for embracing personal problems and negative emotions as a means of finding meaning and happiness in life. Manson argues that most people waste their time and energy on things that don't matter, such as striving for perfection or seeking external validation, instead of focusing on what truly matters to them. He encourages readers to identify their values and priorities, and then give less importance to the things that do not align with those values. The book is written in a humorous and relatable style and aims to help readers live a more fulfilling life by learning to not give a fck about things that do not matter.

Rich Dad Poor Dad

Robert T. Kiyosaki and Sharon Lechter

Rich Dad, Poor Dad is a personal finance book written by Robert Kiyosaki. The book is based on the author's own experiences growing up with two father figures - his biological father (the "poor dad") and his friend's father (the "rich dad") - and the lessons he learned from each of them about money and financial literacy. The rich dad taught Kiyosaki about wealth creation through investing and entrepreneurialism, while the poor dad emphasized the importance of getting a good education and working hard for a steady paycheck. The book argues that traditional methods of building wealth, such as getting a good education and working for a steady salary, are flawed and that the real key to wealth is developing financial literacy and becoming an entrepreneur. The book aims to challenge readers' perceptions about money and encourage them to take control of their financial future.

Psychology of Money

Morgan Housel

The Psychology of Money is a book by Morgan Housel that explores the relationship between money and human behavior. The book argues that our thoughts and actions surrounding money are heavily influenced by our emotions and beliefs, which often have little to do with rational decision making. The author uses stories and historical events to illustrate how our understanding of money is shaped by societal norms and how these norms impact our financial choices. The book also explores how money affects different aspects of our lives, such as relationships, health, and happiness, and how our behavior changes as our financial situation changes. The book aims to help readers understand the psychological and emotional factors that influence their financial decisions and to provide a new perspective on the role of money in their lives.
